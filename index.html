Key Takeaways

● A simple sentence consists of a single independent clause containing a subject and a verb, conveying a complete idea, such as "The dog chased the ball."
● A complex sentence includes one independent clause along with at least one dependent clause, for instance, "While the dog chased the ball, the cat watched from the window."
● A compound sentence contains two or more independent clauses linked by coordinating conjunctions, such as "The dog chased the ball, and the cat climbed a tree."
● A complex-compound sentence combines several independent clauses with at least one dependent clause, like "While the dog chased the ball, the cat climbed a tree, and the bird flew away."

Words that I Learnt from the Story

Steady:
Meaning: Firm, stable, and not subject to change; regular or continuous.
Example: The climber took a deep breath to steady himself before attempting the final ascent to the summit.

Crouched:
Meaning: To bend down low, typically by bending the knees and leaning forward; to squat.
Example: The cat crouched silently in the grass, ready to pounce on the unsuspecting bird.

Fugitive:
Meaning: A person who is fleeing or running away, especially from the law; someone who is in hiding.
Example: The police launched a manhunt to capture the fugitive who escaped from prison last week.
